Did You Know?

"Sus" stands for "suspicious" or "suspect."

A significant portion of Gen Z slang originates from African American Vernacular English (AAVE). Terms like "slay," "bussin'," and "no cap" have roots in AAVE and have been popularized by Gen Z.

"Brain rot" beat 5 other words to become a top Gen Z slang.

Many terms are born and spread through internet memes, social media trends (like TikTok dances), and online gaming communities.

Gen Z uses shortening and abbreviations for efficiency (e.g., "sus," "wyd," "idk").

Words often express strong emotions or exaggeration (e.g., "dead" means extremely funny).

Some Gen Z slang is a revival of older slang or phrases, giving them new life and meanings.

The word "stan" originates from Eminem's 2000 song "Stan."

"Spill the tea" (gossip) is thought to originate from Black drag culture.

"OK Boomer" dismisses viewpoints associated with older generations.

Gen Z slang evolves rapidly, becoming outdated quickly ("cheugy").

Slang can vary regionally within Gen Z.

"Yeet" means to throw something with great force.

Gen Z often uses slang ironically or sarcastically.

Using certain slang can be a way for Gen Z to identify with their peers.

"Vibe check" assesses mood or atmosphere.

"Main character energy" describes acting as a protagonist.

The meaning of Gen Z slang is heavily context-dependent.
